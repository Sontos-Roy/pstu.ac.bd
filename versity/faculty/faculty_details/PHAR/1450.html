Dr. Firoj Ahmed was born at Singra Upazilla of Natore district. He completed SSC from Govt. Laboratory High School, Rajshahi in 1989 and HSC from Rajshahi College in 1991. He Received M. Pharm. Degree from the Department of Pharmacy, University of Dhaka in 1995. He Received PhD in Natural Products Chemistry and Drug design from Chiba University, Japan (under the Supervision of Prof. Dr. Masami Ishibashi) in 2010. He worked as a post-doctoral Fellow (under Prof. Dr. Masami Ishibashi) at Chiba University, Japan in 2013 and 2015.

He joined Beximco Pharma Ltd. as Quality Assurance Officer in 1998 and served there up to 2000. He served as ‘Lecturer’ and ‘Assistant Professor’ at Pharmacy Discipline, Khulna University from 2000-2010. Then he worked as Associate Professor at the Department of Pharmacy, Jagannath University from 2010 to 2013. He joined as a Professor at the Department of Pharmaceutical Chemistry, University of Dhaka in 2013. He has been appointed as Chairman of the Department of Pharmacy, University of Dhaka in September 2020.

Research Interest: Search for bioactive natural products from medicinal plants of Bangladesh. Performed collaborative research works with Prof. Masami Ishibashi, Chiba University supported by MEXT, Japan. Research topic: Search for bioactive natural products targeting signaling molecules in cancer-related biological pathways such as TRAIL, Wnt, and Hedghog signaling pathways.

Publications: Published more than 100 scientific papers home and abroad. Published 10 articles in Oriental Pharmacy and Experimental Medicine, 6 articles in Journal of Natural Products, 5 articles in Biorganic & Medicinal Chemistry Letters, 7 articles in Journal of Natural Medicines, 6 articles in Natural Product Communications, 4 articles in Biorganic & Medicinal Chemistry. Also published articles in Organic Letters, Scientific Reports, ChemBioChem, Organic & Biomolecular Chemistry, ACS Chemical Biology, Molecular BioSystems, Beilstein Journal of Organic Chemistry, Synlett, Phytotherapy Research, Fitoterapia, Phytochemistry Letters, Chemical & Pharmaceutical Bulletin, Pharmaceutical Biology,Die Pharmazie, Food Science Technology Research, etc.

Research Interest

Subject Description Research Interest (Goal, Target Indicator)
1. Search for bio-active natural compounds from natural resoueces 2. Quality evaluation of marketed food products in Bangladesh

1. Medicinal plants of both local and mangrove origins are explored for isolation and characterization of bio-active compounds with antimicrobial and other pharmacological activities.

2. Quality evaluation of marketed food products as pasteurized milk, fruit drinks, oils, ghee, etc., available in local markets in Bangladesh. Samples are purchased from local markets and analyzed for BSTI parameters.
